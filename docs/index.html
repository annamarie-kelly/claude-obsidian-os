01 What it is

A personal OS for the things you've half-thought, half-promised, half-started.

Loops OS is a thinking surface for the loose ends in your head. The "I should..." statements that pile up faster than you can ship them. The tasks that aren't quite tasks yet. The ideas that need a decision before they need a doer.

It captures them, triages them, schedules them, and reflects on them. Then writes everything as plain markdown to a folder on disk, so the day this app dies, your data doesn't.

Most task tools optimize for ticking boxes. Loops OS optimizes for the moment before that: deciding what's worth a box at all, where it sits relative to everything else, and whether you've been spending your week on the things you said mattered.

02 The mental model

A loop moves through four states. The four modes are the surfaces for each.

Capture is the door. Triage is the gate. Backlog is the shelf. Plan is the calendar. Focus is right now. Nothing skips ahead without a decision.

A loop's whole life

Triage answers "what just landed and needs a decision?". One card at a time, three keys (1/2/3) move them out fast. Empty Triage is the goal state.

Backlog answers "what am I committed to but not working on right now?". Items only land here after you accept them in Triage (or mark them P0 to bypass).

Plan is where Backlog items get pushed onto a calendar. Drag to schedule. Focus is the active timeblock or the next one starting today.

03 Capture

Three ways in. All roads lead to 00-Inbox/ and 06-Loops/loops.json.

Capture is intentionally low-friction. The whole system breaks down if you don't trust that the thing you just thought is going to land somewhere safe.

1. In-app: press c

A bottom-of-screen capture bar slides up. Type, hit Enter. Stays open for chained capture (rattle off five things in a row), Esc to close. Empty Enter is a no-op so you don't accidentally tear it down mid-thought.

2. Chrome extension: ⌘⇧L from any tab

Capture from anywhere on the web without leaving the page. The page link auto-fills as a chip. Pick tags, add a note, ship it. Two flavors via a toggle:

  • Loop → Triage: lands in 06-Loops/loops.json with status: triage. For thoughts that need a decision.
  • Clip → Vault: writes a markdown file to 00-Inbox/clips/<date>-<slug>.md with frontmatter. For quotes, articles, things you want to keep without triaging.

Auto-flips to Clip when it detects a text selection on the page (you highlighted something to keep). Right-click any selection → Clip selection to Loops for one-shot capture without opening the popup.

3. MCP integrations

Wire your meeting notes (Granola), your CRM (Affinity), your task tracker (Linear), or your inbox into a /inbox skill. Run /inbox from Claude Code and it pulls today's signal from those sources, drops it into 00-Inbox/ as draft notes, and you triage them in the app. The pull is yours: configure what gets pulled and how.

04 Triage

The gate. One card at a time, three keys, no fence-sitting.

New captures land in Triage with status: triage. They don't appear in any other surface (Focus, Plan, Backlog, Reflect) until you make a decision. This is on purpose. Undecided items shouldn't be allowed to vote.

The three decisions

  • 1 Accept: this matters, into the Backlog with a P-level.
  • 2 Someday: not now, not no, just not this week. Goes to the Someday shelf.
  • 3 Drop: not happening. Closes the loop with closedAs: dropped.

Capacity caps

The triage gate enforces P1 capacity. If accepting this loop would put you over your stakeholder cap (default 8) or self cap (default 5), you get a Capacity Gate modal. You can override (logged to your boundary log) or rerank existing P1s. Caps are configurable per-stakeholder in loops.config.json.

The whole point of capacity caps: you don't accidentally end up with twelve P1:Boss loops because you couldn't say no in the moment. The gate makes the no explicit.

05 Backlog

The shelf. Stored for when you're ready.

Backlog is your active working set. Loops accepted in Triage land here with a P-level and (optionally) a stakeholder. They sit on the shelf without expiring, ready when you are.

P-levels

  • P0: drop everything. Bypasses the triage gate. Use sparingly.
  • P1:<stakeholder> / P1:self: this week's must-do. Capacity-capped.
  • P2: this week if there's room.
  • P3: someday-soon, but on your radar.

Stakeholders are arbitrary. The default is whoever you flagged in loops.config.json, but you can introduce P1:Alice, P1:design, P1:taxes, whatever. Each one gets its own capacity cap.

06 Plan

The week as a canvas. Drag loops onto time.

A Mon-Fri grid. Calendar events render as fixed blocks (your week routes around them). Drag a loop from the sidebar onto a slot to schedule it. Drag again to resize.

If a loop is scheduled but the slot conflicts with a calendar event, the scheduler splits the loop around the conflict (so a 90-minute loop becomes two 45-minute blocks if a 30-minute meeting lands in the middle). The principle: time you've already committed to other things isn't pliable.

Press [ / ] to collapse / expand the loops sidebar. Click any timeblock to drop into Focus on that loop.

07 Focus

One loop on the screen. Everything else hidden.

Focus is right now. The active timeblock auto-selected. Notes inline. Source file rendered. Nothing distracting.

j / k to navigate between scheduled loops. x to mark complete (and start the next one). When you've already decided what to work on, this is the mode you live in.

08 Reflect

30-day pressure heatmap, last-week stats, weekly stakeholder card.

Reflect surfaces the patterns under the activity. Where did the week's pressure go? What's been sitting in Triage too long? What did you ship for whom?

  • Pressure heatmap: 30 days, four colors (sage / mauve / tan / rose) for the four pressure types. Patterns visible at a glance.
  • Last-week triage stats: how many landed, how many accepted, how many dropped, how many overrode capacity.
  • Pattern scan: word-frequency over recent loops surfaces the things that keep coming up.
  • Stakeholder update card: optional weekly draft of what you shipped for your primary stakeholder.
09 The vault

Plain markdown. A folder you own. Future-proof by default.

Loops OS reads and writes a folder structure that happens to be Obsidian-compatible. If the app vanishes tomorrow, your data is still readable in TextEdit, vim, GitHub, anywhere markdown is.

Folder convention

00-Inbox/       capture bucket; daily notes under 00-Inbox/Daily/
01-Building/    projects, technical patterns, playbooks
02-Thinking/    essays, ideas, references
03-Working/     business operations, stakeholders, firm ops
04-Living/      personal life
05-Relating/    people notes
06-Loops/       state files (required): loops.json, calendar-today.json, logs
07-Archive/     done / dead / inactive (never deleted)

Only 06-Loops/ is strictly required. The rest is convention. Override scan folders in loops.config.json if your vault uses different names.

Frontmatter schema

Every note gets standard frontmatter so skills and the scanner know what they're looking at: 

---
created: 2026-05-05
type: project | pattern | playbook | essay | reference | person | loop
status: active | someday | done | archived
shelf-life: foundational | tactical | observational
tags: []
---

Use Obsidian if you want, don't if you don't

The vault drawer (⌘\) browses files, the editor handles [[wikilinks]] with autocomplete, the note reader resolves backlinks. The full edit-create-search-link loop runs without ever opening Obsidian. But if you do use Obsidian, point it at the same folder and both surfaces stay in sync, edit a - [ ] in either place and the other picks it up.

10 Chrome extension

Capture the web in one keystroke.

Press ⌘⇧L from any tab. The capture popup auto-fills the page link and any text you've selected. Talks only to localhost. No external endpoints.

Install

  1. Open chrome://extensions and enable Developer mode.
  2. Click Load unpacked and point at loops-ui/tools/loops-capture-extension/.
  3. Pin the icon. Press ⌘⇧L from any page.

The extension auto-detects which port the loops-ui dev server is running on (probes 3456, 3457, etc.). Move your dev server, the extension re-detects on next capture.

11 Skills

Slash commands for the verbs you actually do.

The vault ships with a .claude/commands/ folder full of slash commands. Each one is a markdown file Claude Code reads as a skill. Run them from anywhere in the vault.

/start
First-run onboarding. Preflight checks, install, seed, dev server.
/triage
Walk the inbox card by card. Route to the right folder with frontmatter.
/distill
Extract claim-style insights from raw input. Updates _patterns.md files.
/reflect
Weekly reflection. Patterns, pressure, what shipped, what stalled.
/loops
Surface open items by domain and priority. The dashboard view.
/commitments
What did you commit to whom. The promise ledger.
/research
Web research woven back into your vault. Connects to existing patterns.
/tend
Terminal-side write surface. The CLI counterpart to the UI.
/priorities
Weekly priorities. P1s, capacity reads, what got bumped.
/review
Weekly review. Whole-vault scan. Rolls up to a stakeholder summary.

Skills are composable. /distill writes to _patterns.md, which /reflect reads. /inbox pulls signal that /triage walks. The vault is the source of truth, skills are verbs.

12 System panel

Press s. See what's connected. See what each connection unlocks.

Most apps make you guess what's wired up. The System panel surfaces every integration as a row, with what it enables. No more "is the calendar connected? is Claude Code installed?" guesswork.

Each row shows:

  • Vault: path, file count, last scan time. → "Read & write surface for everything"
  • Loops config: stakeholder name, caps. → "Stakeholder used in P1:<name>, caps gate triage"
  • Calendar: event count, last sync. → "Plan view shows fixed blocks"
  • Claude Code CLI: detected? → "Chat panel + skills" (or install hint)
  • Obsidian: env var set? → "Open-in-Obsidian buttons in detail drawer"
  • Chrome extension: last capture? → "⌘⇧L capture from any tab"
  • MCP / Skills: skill count, /inbox present? → "/inbox, /triage etc. via /start"
13 First launch

From paste-the-installer to ready-to-use in under three minutes.

Five gates, each with a graceful fallback. By the end you have a server running, a vault seeded with examples, and a tour walking you through the four modes.

  1. Installer: curl -fsSL ... | bash detects your platform, checks Node 20+ and git, clones the repo to ~/loops-os, hands off.
  2. Preflight: npm run start runs system checks (Node version, port 3456, Claude Code CLI, Obsidian).
  3. Setup prompt: one question, optional. "Primary stakeholder name? (Enter to skip)". Writes to loops.config.json.
  4. Dev server + browser: starts Next.js, opens http://localhost:3456 in your default browser.
  5. Ritual + tour: a fullscreen ritual asks "what are you trying to ship in the next two weeks?", then a quiet bottom-right card walks you through Triage → Backlog → Plan → Focus.

15 Setup

Paste one line. The rest is automatic.

You need Node 20+ and git. Everything else is optional. The installer detects what's missing and tells you how to fix it.

Install (mac · linux)
curl -fsSL https://raw.githubusercontent.com/annamarie-kelly/loops-os/main/install.sh | bash

The script is short and readable. Review it on GitHub before piping if you're cautious. It clones the repo to ~/loops-os (override with LOOPS_OS_DIR=/path) then hands off to npm run start, which runs preflight, installs deps, prompts for setup, starts the dev server, and opens the browser.

Or, manually

git clone https://github.com/annamarie-kelly/loops-os.git
cd loops-os/loops-ui
npm run start

Windows

The installer doesn't support Windows yet. Use the manual path above. A native install.ps1 is on the roadmap.

16 Configuration

Two files. Both optional. Defaults work.

loops.config.json

Stakeholder name, capacity caps, scan folders, scanner stakeholder keywords. Read at build time, restart npm run dev after editing. 

{
  "vault": {
    "scanFolders": ["00-Inbox", "01-Building", "02-Thinking", "03-Working", "04-Living"],
    "inboxFile": "00-Inbox/manual-loops.md"
  },
  "stakeholder": {
    "name": "Boss",
    "tag": "Boss",
    "capacityMax": 8,
    "weeklySummary": true
  },
  "self": { "capacityMax": 5 },
  "priorityCaps": { "P1Flat": 8, "P2Flat": 20 }
}

.env.local

Environment overrides. All optional. Defaults to the bundled vault-template/. 

# Path to your vault. Defaults to ../vault-template
LOOPS_UI_VAULT_ROOT=/absolute/path/to/your/vault

# Obsidian vault folder name (enables "Open in Obsidian" buttons)
NEXT_PUBLIC_OBSIDIAN_VAULT=your-vault-name

# Folder where agent specs live (default: 01-Building/Agent Specs)
LOOPS_SPECS_FOLDER=01-Building/Agent Specs

See loops-ui/.env.example in the repo for the full list with descriptions.

17 Why this shape

Triage is the gate. Backlog is the shelf. Focus is right now.

The architecture is opinionated. Here's why.

Most task tools optimize for ticking boxes. The hard part isn't ticking the box, it's deciding which boxes are worth having. So Loops OS makes the decision the visible thing. Capture is cheap. Triage is mandatory. Anything that hasn't been triaged doesn't get to vote in any other view. The undecided don't get a seat at the table.

Capacity caps are not productivity hacks, they're discipline made structural. If your P1:Boss bucket already has eight items and a ninth shows up, the system makes you choose. Either rerank, defer, or override (and log that override). Most "I have too much going on" weeks come from accepting things in the moment that you wouldn't have accepted on reflection. The cap forces the reflection.

Plain markdown is ownership. The day this app is unmaintained, your data is still readable. The day you want to switch to a different tool, you don't migrate. The day a skill writes a note, you can read it in any text editor. Lock-in is the silent killer of personal-system tools.

Skills are composable verbs. /triage is a verb that operates on 00-Inbox/. /distill is a verb that operates on a raw note. /reflect is a verb that operates on the last week's loops + checkpoints. They share the vault as a substrate. New verb? Add a markdown file to .claude/commands/. The system grows by addition, not by app updates.

Empty Triage is the goal state. Not "ten loops scheduled." Not "five things shipped today." A clean inbox where every undecided item has been decided. — the design principle

Focus is right now because right now is when the work happens. Plan is for setting up right now. Backlog is for staging right now. Triage is for protecting right now from things that don't deserve to be there yet. Reflect is for noticing what right-now has been. The whole stack is in service of one moment: the actual one you're in.
