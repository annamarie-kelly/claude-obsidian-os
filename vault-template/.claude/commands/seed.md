# Seed — Populate Your Digital Garden

Interview me to learn about my life, then create starter notes across the vault.

## Step 1: Ask me about my life

Ask these questions one section at a time. Don't rush — let me ramble. The gold is in the details.

**Creating:**
- What projects are you actively working on?
- What's the current state of each? What are you stuck on?
- Any tools, materials, or techniques you're experimenting with?

**Thinking:**
- What are you reading or learning right now?
- Any books, articles, podcasts, or courses that shaped how you think?
- Ideas you keep coming back to? Intellectual obsessions?

**Living:**
- What life admin is on your plate? (health, finances, home, errands)
- Any upcoming appointments, deadlines, or recurring obligations?
- Goals you're working toward this year?

**Relating:**
- Who are the key people in your life right now? (collaborators, mentors, friends, family)
- Anyone you want to stay in better touch with?
- Who's taught you something important recently?

**Career (cross-cutting):**
- How does career show up across your life right now?
- Any career-related projects, contacts, or goals?

## Step 2: Create notes from what I shared

After the interview, propose a batch of notes to create. Group them:

### Projects → `01-Building/`
For each active project, create a note using the project template. Fill in what I told you — goal, current state, open questions, first log entry.

### References & Ideas → `02-Thinking/`
For books, articles, or intellectual threads, create reference notes. For recurring ideas, create spark notes in `00-Inbox/` for later triage.

### Life Admin → `04-Living/`
Create notes for active life admin areas (health, finances, etc.) with `- [ ]` tasks for anything I mentioned needing to do.

### People → `05-Relating/`
For key people, create person notes with context, how I know them, and what they've taught me.

### Patterns → seeds
If anything I said sounds like an emerging insight or belief, propose it as a seed pattern (claim-style title). Ask before creating these — they should feel true.

## Step 3: Confirm before creating

Show me the full list of notes you plan to create, organized by folder. Include a preview of each note's content. Let me edit, add, or remove before you write anything.

## Rules

- Use Templater-style frontmatter matching the vault's templates
- Add `#career` tag to anything career-related
- Use `[[wikilinks]]` to link between the notes you're creating
- Add `- [ ]` tasks for any action items I mention
- Don't over-polish my words — keep my voice
- It's okay to create a lot of notes. A well-seeded garden grows faster.
